Our Mission

Understanding Tissue
Function & Healing

Our research integrates multiple scales of investigation, from molecular mechanisms to whole-tissue biomechanics. We combine cutting-edge technology with fundamental science to address critical questions in musculoskeletal health.

By understanding healthy tissue function and identifying changes that occur with injury or disease, we provide evidence-based guidelines for improved treatment strategies that can be implemented in clinical practice.

Comprehensive Approach

Multiple scales of investigation from molecular to whole-tissue biomechanics.

Translational Focus

Evidence-based guidelines for improved treatment strategies in clinical practice.

Innovative Methods

From wearable sensors to sophisticated preclinical animal model testing.

Collaborative Environment

Working at the intersection of engineering, biology, and medicine.

Research Areas

Current Focus

Our lab pursues innovative research across multiple domains of musculoskeletal health, combining fundamental science with translational applications.

01 / 04

Tendon Biomechanics

Investigating the mechanical properties and healing response of tendons to develop improved rehabilitation protocols and treatment strategies for tendon injuries.

Mechanical Testing Rehabilitation Injury Response
02 / 04

Muscle Function

Examining muscle structure-function relationships and adaptation to injury, exercise, and aging using advanced biomechanical and imaging techniques.

Structure-Function Imaging Adaptation
03 / 04

Wearable Technology

Developing and validating wearable sensors for real-time monitoring of movement and tissue loading in clinical and athletic populations.

Sensors Real-time Monitoring Athletics
04 / 04

Preclinical Models

Utilizing animal models to understand fundamental mechanisms of tissue healing and to test novel therapeutic interventions in controlled settings.

Animal Models Therapeutics Mechanism Studies

Research Focus

Dr. Reiter's research is focused on optimizing treatment solutions for musculoskeletal injuries. With a primary focus on tendon and ligament, his research program seeks to understand healthy tissue function and structure, assess changes that occur in injury/disease, and provide guidelines for improved treatment strategies. His group uses a diverse set of techniques from wearable sensors in humans to biological, structural, and mechanical testing in preclinical animal models.
